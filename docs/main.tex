\begin{abstract}
COMPASS estimates gene-by-context mediated trait contributions from GWAS
summary statistics, SNP-to-gene annotations, and linkage disequilibrium (LD).
We applied a non-negative nuclear-norm relaxation of the COMPASS model to
Alzheimer disease (AD) summary statistics using SingleBrain top eQTL
annotations and UK Biobank LD. The genome-wide design contained 76,101 aligned
variants, 15,363 genes, and eight annotation contexts including an intercept and
seven brain cell-type annotations. The regularization parameter was selected by
full leave-one-chromosome cross-validation. Cross-validation favored the
strongest penalties tested; the selected model was the all-zero mediated fit.
Consequently, the primary analysis did not implicate global or cell-type-specific
AD genes, and ordered g:Profiler gene-set enrichment had no positive ranked gene
list to test. A weaker, non-selected sensitivity fit at $\lambda=100$ produced
four very small positive gene scores, but these did not yield significant
enrichment and should not be interpreted as discoveries.
\end{abstract}

\section{Model}

Let $\chi_j^2$ be the marginal GWAS statistic for variant $j$ and let
$a_{jkl} \geq 0$ denote the annotation linking variant $j$ to gene $k$ in
context $l$. The context index can represent a biochemical mechanism, an
intercept, or a cell type. LD-smoothed annotations are
\begin{equation}
    T_{jkl} = \sum_m r_{jm}^2 a_{mkl},
\end{equation}
where $r_{jm}^2$ is squared LD. COMPASS models the expected summary statistic as
\begin{equation}
    E[\chi_j^2] =
    1 + N_j \left(\sum_{k,l} B_{kl} T_{jkl} + \tau l_j \right),
\end{equation}
where $B_{kl} \geq 0$ is the mediated contribution of gene $k$ through context
$l$, $\tau \geq 0$ is a residual polygenic component, and
$l_j=\sum_m r_{jm}^2$ is the LD score. The fitted objective is
\begin{equation}
    \min_{B \geq 0,\tau \geq 0}
    \frac{1}{J}\sum_j w_j
    \left\{\chi_j^2 - 1 - N_j \left(\sum_{k,l} B_{kl}T_{jkl}+\tau l_j\right)\right\}^2
    + \lambda \|B\|_* .
\end{equation}
The nuclear norm encourages shared low-rank gene-by-context structure while the
non-negativity constraint prevents LD-correlated neighboring genes from fitting
the same signal through cancelling positive and negative coefficients. The
strict rank-1 alternative, $B=s w^\top$, was retained only as an optimization
baseline because it imposes a single global context profile on all genes.

\section{Data and implementation}

We used the Jansen et al. AD GWAS summary statistics distributed as
\texttt{AD\_sumstats\_Jansenetal\_2019sept.txt.gz}. Variant-to-gene annotations
were built from SingleBrain top eQTL associations for astrocytes, endothelial
cells, excitatory neurons, inhibitory neurons, microglia, oligodendrocytes, and
OPCs, with one intercept context per variant-gene pair. LD was assembled from
all downloaded UK Biobank LD \texttt{.npz} blocks and their metadata sidecars.
Sample sizes were read from the GWAS file; runs without known sample sizes are
rejected.

The cached genome-wide design contained 76,101 variants and 122,904
gene-context parameters. The sparse annotation matrix had 166,253 nonzero
entries and the aligned LD matrix had 7,829,001 nonzero entries. Initial LD
assembly was the dominant setup cost, but the cached dataset subsequently loaded
in less than one second. The final high-penalty cross-validation run used a GPU
and completed the fit stage in 144 seconds.

\section{Lambda selection}

The penalty $\lambda$ was selected by full leave-one-chromosome
cross-validation. For each chromosome, the model was fit on the remaining 21
chromosomes and evaluated on the held-out chromosome. The final grid was widened
after earlier grids selected their upper endpoint. The broad grid showed that
the optimum had reached the zero-mediated plateau (Table~\ref{tab:lambda}).

The selected run used $\lambda=100000$ and produced $B=0$ for all 15,363 genes
and all eight contexts. The total mediated score, the number of positive
gene-context entries, and the number of positive genes were all zero. Thus the
primary COMPASS fit does not support a detectable mediated AD component from
these top-eQTL annotations under the present objective and weighting scheme.

\section{Alternating rank-1 baseline}

The alternating rank-1 optimizer was evaluated with the same
leave-one-chromosome selection scheme on a lower penalty grid. Its held-out
errors were on the order of $10^{16}$, compared with approximately 164 for the
nuclear-norm fit. The resulting rank-1 matrix was dense across essentially all
genes and did not provide a useful sparse or stable AD gene ranking. This
baseline is therefore not useful for the present data except as a negative
control demonstrating the practical value of the convex relaxation.

\section{Gene ranking and enrichment}

For the primary CV-selected fit, global gene scores were defined as row sums of
$B$, and cell-type-specific scores were defined by the corresponding columns of
$B$. Because every entry of $B$ was zero, there were no positive global,
astrocyte, endothelial, excitatory neuron, inhibitory neuron, microglia,
oligodendrocyte, or OPC gene lists. Ordered g:Profiler enrichment was run with
the 15,363 assayed genes as the custom background, but no primary enrichment
test was possible because the positive ranked lists were empty.

As a sensitivity analysis, the adjacent weaker fit at $\lambda=100$ produced
four positive global genes: \textit{ZNF222}, \textit{ZBTB11-AS1},
\textit{CCDC153}, and \textit{LCN10}. Their total scores were very small
($3.0\times10^{-6}$, $2.0\times10^{-6}$, $1.7\times10^{-6}$, and
$1.3\times10^{-6}$, respectively), and the same four genes led each cell-type
ranking with nearly proportional weights. Ordered g:Profiler returned no
meaningful enrichment for this sensitivity fit; the top reported terms had
$p=1.0$ and query size one. These genes are therefore best viewed as numerical
edge effects near the zero solution, not as AD gene discoveries.

\section{Discussion}

The full chromosome-held-out lambda selection answered the main modeling
question: the selected penalty lies on a broad null plateau, so the current AD
analysis does not identify robust global or cell-type-specific mediated genes.
The earlier, narrower grids were not wide enough because their selected lambdas
were upper-boundary values. Extending the grid to $10^5$ showed that increasing
regularization improves held-out prediction until the mediated component is
removed entirely.

This result does not rule out mediated AD architecture. It shows that this
particular combination of top eQTL annotations, UK Biobank LD, WOLS weighting,
and nuclear-norm penalty does not improve chromosome-held-out prediction over
the residual LD-score component. Natural next checks are alternative annotation
scalings, richer SNP-to-gene annotations than one top eQTL per gene and cell
type, calibrated LDSC-style regression weights, and validation against known AD
loci before interpreting any weaker-penalty gene rankings.
